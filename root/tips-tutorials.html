Tips & Tutorials

Here are some tips I've picked up along the way

  • One of the best ways to get started on a jigsaw puzzle is to begin with the edges. Sorting out all the edge pieces helps you build the frame, giving you a clear boundary to work within. As you go, sort pieces by color, pattern, or distinct features—this makes it easier to identify where certain sections might fit.

  • Working on small, manageable sections rather than trying to force random pieces can help you stay focused and make steady progress. If the puzzle image has bold colors or unique shapes, start there; they’re usually the easiest to place.

  • Lighting and workspace also make a big difference. A well-lit area with enough space to spread out all the pieces helps reduce eye strain and makes color and pattern differences more visible.

  • Using trays, bowls, or sorting boxes to separate pieces by type or color can keep things organized and save time in the long run.

  • And finally, be patient—taking short breaks can give you a fresh perspective and prevent frustration. Sometimes just stepping away and returning with clear eyes helps you spot the piece you’ve been searching for.
